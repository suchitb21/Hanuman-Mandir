Our History

Tilaknagar emerged as a housing colony in 1954, when MHADA envisioned dignified homes for Mumbai’s working class. Life then was governed by strict rules, even simple home modifications required permission. Yet, amidst these limitations, residents nurtured a shared desire for spiritual comfort, unity, and a sacred space they could call their own.

Driven by faith and perseverance, a group of respected seniors took responsibility for building a Mandir within the colony. Despite resistance, legal hurdles, and repeated demolition attempts under police supervision, the community stood united. Their unwavering belief in Lord Hanuman transformed struggle into strength and preserved the Mandir’s sanctity.

Today, this Mandir stands as one of Tilaknagar’s oldest and most revered spiritual landmarks. Its enduring presence reflects the deep faith of countless devotees who find peace, hope, and emotional grounding here. The Mandir has grown not just in structure, but in hearts, devotion, and collective belonging.

The Mandir houses shrines of Lord Hanuman, Lord Ganapati, Lord Shankar, Maa Parvati, Lord Dattaguru, and Maa Shitaladevi. Daily pujas, special rituals, Abhisheks, and Laghurudras are conducted with utmost adherence to tradition. Devotees are lovingly guided for individual worship needs.

Major festivals such as Mahashivratri, Hanuman Jayanti, and Shardiya Navratri are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Events include mass Laghurudra, palkhi processions, bhajans, cultural programs, Garba, Jagrata, and community favorites like the Tilaknagar Home Minister, creating joy for all generations.

Beyond devotion, the Mandir actively serves society through satsangs, Baal Sanskaar, and initiatives like the Renuka Vadhuwar Mandal, offering marriage support and free counseling. With humility and openness, we welcome suggestions from all devotees as we strive together to enhance spiritual and social service.

Our Mentor

During its formative years, the Mandir was lovingly nurtured under the guidance of Late Shri Pramod Shirwalkar Ji, the revered Founder Father of this holy place. As the elected leader of the locality, he fulfilled the heartfelt aspirations of devotees and played a pivotal role in the Mandir’s growth while safeguarding its sanctity.

The Trustees remain deeply grateful for his selfless service and solemnly pledge to carry forward his noble vision, ensuring that this sacred abode continues to be a place where prayers are heard and blessings flow abundantly from Lord Hanuman.
